### The Edwards Aquifer and San Antonio's Hard Reality

San Antonio's water supply, primarily sourced from the vast Edwards Aquifer, is a natural treasure, but it comes with a well-known characteristic: significant hardness. Local water quality reports consistently show levels averaging **15-20 grains per gallon (GPG)**. To put that in perspective, anything above 7 GPG is considered very hard. This high mineral content, rich in calcium and magnesium, isn't just an abstract number; it impacts daily life.

Hard water leads to:
*   Unsightly scale buildup on fixtures, dishes, and shower doors.
*   Reduced efficiency and lifespan of water-using appliances like water heaters, dishwashers, and washing machines.
*   Drier skin and hair, and duller, scratchy laundry.
*   Increased soap and detergent consumption to achieve lather.

This is the challenging environment in which companies like **Water Softener Installation** operate, providing essential services that significantly improve the quality of life for San Antonio households.
