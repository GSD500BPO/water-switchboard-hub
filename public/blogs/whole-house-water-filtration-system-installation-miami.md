### The Miami Water Story: What's in Your Tap?

Understanding what's in your water is the first step toward improving it. Miami's water, drawn from the Biscayne Aquifer, is naturally rich in dissolved minerals, particularly calcium and magnesium from limestone. This is what leads to "hard water" – manifesting as soap scum, stubborn residue on dishes, and reduced efficiency and lifespan for appliances like water heaters and dishwashers.

Beyond minerals, the city's water treatment process involves chlorination, an essential step for disinfection. However, this often leaves tap water with a distinct, sometimes unpleasant, chlorine odor and taste. Additionally, during warmer months, the aquifer can experience algae blooms, which, while not always harmful, can contribute to earthy or musty tastes and odors in the water.
