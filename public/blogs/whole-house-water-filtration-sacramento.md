### Sacramento's Water Story: A River City's Challenge

The inherent softness of Sacramento's river water is often a welcome characteristic, meaning fewer issues with mineral buildup in pipes and appliances compared to harder water regions. However, the very rivers that provide our drinking water are also arteries of a vast agricultural landscape. This proximity means that during certain times of the year, runoff from farms can carry pesticides, herbicides, and other agricultural byproducts into the water supply. While municipal treatment plants do an excellent job of making water safe to drink according to federal standards, many homeowners desire an additional layer of protection against these potential trace contaminants and other aesthetic issues like taste or odor.
