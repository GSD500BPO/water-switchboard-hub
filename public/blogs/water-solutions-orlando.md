### The Orlando Water Story: Hardness, Sulfur, and Iron

Anyone who's lived in Orlando for a while can likely attest to the region's unique water profile. Primarily sourced from groundwater, our water often carries elevated levels of minerals. The most noticeable culprits are:

*   **Hardness:** High mineral content, particularly calcium and magnesium, leads to "hard water." This isn't just an inconvenience; it can cause scale buildup in pipes and appliances, reducing their lifespan and efficiency. Think crusty faucets, dull laundry, and soap scum that seems impossible to scrub away.
*   **Sulfur Odor:** Many Orlando residents are familiar with the "rotten egg" smell emanating from their taps. This is typically caused by hydrogen sulfide gas, a natural byproduct of decaying organic matter in groundwater. While generally not harmful, it's certainly unpleasant.
*   **Iron Content:** Elevated iron can lead to reddish-brown staining on fixtures, laundry, and even give drinking water a metallic taste. Like hardness, it's a common characteristic of Florida's groundwater.

These challenges are why a generic, one-size-fits-all approach to water treatment simply won't cut it in Orlando. Local expertise is key.
