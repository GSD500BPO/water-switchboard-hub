### Understanding Austin's Water Challenges

The "moderately hard" label for Austin's water means it contains elevated levels of dissolved minerals, primarily calcium and magnesium. While these minerals are not harmful to drink, their effects are noticeable:
*   **Scale Buildup:** White, crusty deposits on faucets, showerheads, and inside pipes and water-using appliances (like water heaters and dishwashers).
*   **Soap Scum:** Less effective lathering from soaps and detergents, leaving behind residues on dishes, clothes, and skin.
*   **Dry Skin and Hair:** Mineral residue can cling to skin and hair, potentially leading to dryness or dullness.
*   **Appliance Efficiency:** Over time, scale buildup can reduce the efficiency and lifespan of water heaters, dishwashers, and washing machines.

Furthermore, the seasonal fluctuations in mineral content, influenced by rainfall and river flow, can mean that water quality isn't always consistent, presenting a dynamic challenge for homeowners.
