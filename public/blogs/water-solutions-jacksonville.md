### Unpacking Jacksonville's Water Profile: Sulfur, Iron, and Calcium

Let's delve a little deeper into what these minerals mean for your home:

*   **Calcium (Hard Water):** The most common culprit, calcium leads to "hard water." You've likely seen its effects: soap scum in showers, white scale buildup on faucets and dishwashers, and clothes that feel stiff after washing. Hard water can also reduce the efficiency of water heaters and other appliances, costing you more in energy and premature replacements. Water Solutions offers **water softener installation** as a direct answer to this pervasive problem, working to remove these hardening minerals and prevent scale buildup.
*   **Iron:** While not harmful to consume in typical amounts, iron in water can be a nuisance. It often imparts a metallic taste, and perhaps more noticeably, leaves reddish-brown stains on fixtures, laundry, and even sidewalks. High iron levels can also cause cloudy water.
*   **Sulfur:** The presence of hydrogen sulfide gas is often the source of that unmistakable "rotten egg" smell in your water. While generally not a health risk, it's certainly unpleasant and can make drinking and bathing an undesirable experience.
