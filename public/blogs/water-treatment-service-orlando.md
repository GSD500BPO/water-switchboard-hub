**The Orlando Water Story: Hardness, Sulfur, and Iron**

Central Florida's water primarily comes from groundwater sources, specifically the Floridan Aquifer. While this provides a generally safe supply, it also comes with a distinct set of characteristics that can impact daily life.

*   **Elevated Hardness:** This is perhaps the most common complaint. Hard water, rich in dissolved minerals like calcium and magnesium, leads to limescale buildup on fixtures, inside pipes, and within appliances. This not only makes cleaning a chore but can also reduce the efficiency and lifespan of water heaters, dishwashers, and washing machines. Soap struggles to lather, leaving skin feeling dry and hair dull.
*   **Sulfur Odor:** Many Orlando residents are all too familiar with the "rotten egg" smell emanating from their faucets. This is caused by hydrogen sulfide gas, a natural byproduct of certain bacteria interacting with groundwater minerals. While generally not a health risk, it's undeniably unpleasant.
*   **Iron Content:** Iron in water, even at low levels, can be a nuisance. It causes reddish-brown staining on sinks, toilets, laundry, and dishes. Over time, it can also lead to sediment buildup and a metallic taste.
