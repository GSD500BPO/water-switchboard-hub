### Understanding Chicago's Water Landscape

While the City of Chicago Department of Water Management works diligently to treat and deliver safe water, the journey from Lake Michigan to your home can introduce contaminants. Chlorine is used for disinfection, which, while effective against bacteria, can leave an undesirable taste and odor. More significantly, the estimated 390,000 lead service lines still in place throughout Chicago pose a genuine risk of lead leaching into drinking water, especially in homes built before 1986. Even though the city is working on replacement programs, protecting your household remains a proactive step many Chicagoans are choosing to take.
