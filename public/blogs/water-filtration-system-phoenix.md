### Understanding Phoenix's Water: From River to Residence

Phoenix primarily draws its water from the Salt and Verde Rivers, supplemented by the mighty Colorado River via the Central Arizona Project. While these sources provide a reliable supply, the desert climate itself plays a significant role in water quality. The natural mineral content in the region, coupled with the long distances water travels through distribution systems, often leads to elevated levels of Total Dissolved Solids (TDS).

For Phoenix residents, elevated TDS often translates to issues like hard water spots on dishes and fixtures, a potential mineral taste, and concerns about the long-term impact on appliances. This is where professional water treatment becomes not just a luxury, but a practical necessity for many.
