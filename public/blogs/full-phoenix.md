### Understanding Phoenix's Water Challenges

Phoenix's water journey is quite distinct. The city primarily draws its water from a combination of the Salt and Verde Rivers and the Colorado River. While these sources provide essential hydration for the desert city, they also contribute to specific water quality characteristics. The desert climate itself leads to higher evaporation rates, which can concentrate minerals in the water. This, coupled with a long distribution system, often results in elevated levels of Total Dissolved Solids (TDS) and noticeable hard water issues. Homeowners frequently report concerns about scale buildup on fixtures, dry skin and hair, and an unpleasant taste or odor from their tap water.
