### Atlanta's Water Story: Soft Water with a Catch

For many Atlanta residents, the good news is that the city's water is generally considered soft. This means fewer issues with hard water scale buildup in pipes and appliances compared to other regions. However, the vital process of disinfection, which uses chlorine to eliminate harmful pathogens, leaves behind a common byproduct: **chlorine disinfection byproducts (DBPs)**.

While necessary for public health, DBPs can contribute to an undesirable taste and odor in tap water. More importantly, long-term exposure to certain DBPs has been linked to potential health concerns, prompting a growing number of Atlantans to consider advanced filtration options. It's a classic trade-off in public health: ensure water is safe from immediate biological threats, then address the secondary concerns that arise from the treatment process itself.
