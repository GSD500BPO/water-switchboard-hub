### The Jacksonville Water Story: Sulfur, Iron, and Calcium

Jacksonville's water, drawn primarily from the Floridan Aquifer, is rich in natural minerals. While these aren't typically health hazards, they do present common aesthetic and functional challenges:

*   **Sulfur:** Often the culprit behind that tell-tale "rotten egg" smell. While usually harmless, it's certainly unpleasant and can make drinking water unappealing.
*   **Iron:** This mineral can manifest as reddish-brown stains in sinks, toilets, and laundry, and sometimes impart a metallic taste to the water. It can also cause buildup in pipes over time.
*   **Calcium:** A major contributor to **hard water**. High calcium levels lead to limescale buildup on fixtures and appliances, reducing their efficiency and lifespan. It also makes soaps and detergents less effective, leading to dull laundry and less satisfying showers.

These are the everyday realities that drive many Jacksonville homeowners and businesses to seek professional water treatment.
