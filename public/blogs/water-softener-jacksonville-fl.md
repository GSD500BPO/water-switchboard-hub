### The Jacksonville Water Story: More Than Meets the Eye (and Nose)

Imagine stepping into your shower, only to be greeted by a faint, unpleasant "rotten egg" smell. This is often the tell-tale sign of sulfur, a common complaint among Jacksonville homeowners. Beyond the odor, iron can manifest as reddish-brown stains on fixtures, laundry, and even affect the taste of your morning coffee. But perhaps the most pervasive issue, due to high levels of naturally occurring calcium, is **hard water**.

Hard water leaves behind mineral deposits, or "scale," on everything from showerheads and faucets to water heaters and dishwashers. This not only reduces the efficiency and lifespan of appliances but can also leave skin feeling dry and hair dull after showering. These are not minor inconveniences; they impact daily life and can add up to significant expenses over time.
