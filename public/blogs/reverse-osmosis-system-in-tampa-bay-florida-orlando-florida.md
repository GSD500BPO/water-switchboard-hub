### Orlando's Unique Water Woes

Before diving into solutions, it's essential to understand the problem. Orlando's groundwater, while generally safe to drink, presents several common challenges for residents:

*   **Elevated Hardness:** This is perhaps the most widespread issue. High mineral content (calcium and magnesium) leads to limescale buildup on fixtures, inside pipes, and within appliances like water heaters and dishwashers. This not only shortens appliance lifespan but also reduces their efficiency and leaves behind unsightly soap scum.
*   **Sulfur Odor:** Many Orlando residents are familiar with the "rotten egg" smell emanating from their taps, especially with hot water. This is typically caused by hydrogen sulfide gas, often a byproduct of bacteria in groundwater or water heaters.
*   **Iron Content:** Iron in water can lead to reddish-brown staining on sinks, toilets, and laundry, and can also impart a metallic taste to drinking water.

These issues, while not typically health hazards, significantly impact comfort, aesthetics, and the longevity of home infrastructure.
