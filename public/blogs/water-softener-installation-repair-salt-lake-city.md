### Salt Lake City's Water Profile: A Hard Truth

The Wasatch Front's iconic landscape, fed by majestic mountain snowmelt and supplemented by underground aquifers, provides our city with its water. While pure and refreshing, this natural journey imbues the water with minerals, primarily calcium and magnesium, resulting in a moderate hardness level. On average, Salt Lake City water measures between **12-15 grains per gallon (gpg)**. For context, anything above 7 gpg is generally considered hard, and our local water easily falls into this category.

This isn't just a technical detail; it translates to tangible problems. Appliances like dishwashers, washing machines, and water heaters work harder, accumulating scale buildup that shortens their lifespan and reduces efficiency. Showerheads clog, soap lathers poorly, and skin and hair can feel dry and brittle after washing. It's a pervasive issue that often leads residents to seek effective water treatment solutions.
