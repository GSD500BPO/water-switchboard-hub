### The LA Water Story: Hardness, Distance, and Demand

Los Angeles relies heavily on imported water, which brings with it a unique set of characteristics. Water from the Colorado River, for instance, is naturally rich in dissolved minerals like calcium and magnesium. These minerals are the primary culprits behind what we commonly call "hard water." While not a health risk, hard water can lead to scale buildup in pipes and appliances, leave spots on dishes, and make soap less effective, impacting everything from your morning shower to industrial processes.

The vast distances water travels to reach LA homes and businesses also play a role. The journey through miles of infrastructure can introduce sediment or alter water characteristics, making localized treatment solutions increasingly appealing.
