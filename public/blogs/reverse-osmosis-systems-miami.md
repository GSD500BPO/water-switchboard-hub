### Unpacking Miami's Water Challenges

The Biscayne Aquifer, while a vital resource, comes with its own set of characteristics that impact home water quality. High levels of **chlorine** are commonly used in municipal treatment to ensure safety, but this often leaves an unpleasant taste and odor. Beyond that, the limestone bedrock of the region contributes to hard water, rich in **minerals** that lead to scale buildup in pipes and appliances, and leave unsightly spots on dishes. Add to this the occasional presence of **algae byproducts**, especially during warmer months, and it's clear why many Miamians seek additional home filtration.
