**The Jacksonville Water Story: Sulfur, Iron, and Hardness**

As you might already know if you live here, Jacksonville's groundwater from the Floridan Aquifer contains natural minerals including **sulfur**, **iron**, and **calcium** that affect taste and appearance. Sulfur often manifests as a "rotten egg" smell, particularly noticeable in hot water. Iron can leave reddish-brown stains on fixtures, laundry, and even affect the taste of beverages. Meanwhile, high levels of calcium and magnesium contribute to hard water, leading to soap scum, reduced appliance efficiency, and dry skin and hair. These are not typically health hazards but can be a persistent nuisance that degrades quality of life and shortens the lifespan of plumbing and appliances.
