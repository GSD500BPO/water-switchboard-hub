### Understanding Jacksonville's Water: The Floridan Aquifer's Influence

The Floridan Aquifer, an underground system of porous limestone, is rich in dissolved minerals. This geological reality means that many Jacksonville households experience what's commonly known as "hard water" due to high levels of **calcium**. Hard water can lead to stubborn scale buildup on fixtures, inside pipes, and within water-using appliances, reducing their lifespan and efficiency. Beyond calcium, the aquifer can also introduce **sulfur**, often manifesting as a "rotten egg" smell, and **iron**, which can cause reddish-brown staining on sinks, toilets, and laundry, as well as an metallic taste.

Addressing these issues requires targeted approaches, and Donovanac's service offerings are specifically tailored to neutralize these common annoyances.
