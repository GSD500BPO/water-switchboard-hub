### Orlando's Unique Water Story: From Groundwater to Your Tap

Central Florida's water supply primarily comes from groundwater sources, which, while abundant, naturally contain a higher concentration of minerals and compounds. This leads to the specific challenges many Orlando residents face:

*   **Elevated Hardness**: Hard water minerals, primarily calcium and magnesium, lead to limescale buildup on fixtures, reduced soap lather, dry skin and hair, and decreased lifespan for water-using appliances.
*   **Sulfur Odor**: The distinctive "rotten egg" smell often points to hydrogen sulfide gas in the water, a common issue in groundwater, making showering and drinking unpleasant.
*   **Iron Content**: Dissolved iron can cause reddish-brown stains on sinks, toilets, and laundry, and can also impart a metallic taste to drinking water.

These issues aren't just minor inconveniences; they impact everything from the efficiency of your dishwasher to the taste of your morning coffee. Addressing them effectively requires specialized knowledge and systems designed for these specific regional conditions.
