**The Reality of Orlando's Water Quality**

As a major metropolitan area relying heavily on groundwater, Orlando's water supply naturally contains elevated levels of certain minerals and compounds. The primary concerns for homeowners typically include:

*   **Elevated Hardness:** High mineral content, primarily calcium and magnesium, leads to limescale buildup on fixtures, inside pipes, and within water-using appliances. This can reduce efficiency, increase energy bills, and shorten the lifespan of everything from water heaters to dishwashers.
*   **Sulfur Odor:** Often described as a "rotten egg" smell, this is caused by hydrogen sulfide gas. While not typically a health concern, it's unpleasant and can permeate your entire home.
*   **Iron Content:** Iron in water can leave reddish-brown stains on sinks, toilets, and laundry. It can also give water a metallic taste and contribute to bacterial growth.

These are not isolated incidents but common characteristics of the region's groundwater. Understanding these local nuances is crucial for any effective water treatment plan.
