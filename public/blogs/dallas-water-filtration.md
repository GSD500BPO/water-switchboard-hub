### Navigating Dallas's Unique Water Landscape

The water supplied to Dallas homes, primarily sourced from area reservoirs, comes with its own set of characteristics. A key factor is its **moderate hardness**, typically ranging from 8 to 12 grains per gallon. While not the hardest water in the nation, this level is certainly high enough to cause noticeable issues for homeowners. Think stubborn limescale accumulating on fixtures, spotty dishes, inefficient appliances, and dry, itchy skin after showering.

Beyond hardness, Dallas water also experiences **seasonal chlorine fluctuations**. Chlorine is essential for disinfecting public water supplies, but its presence can impart an unpleasant taste and odor to drinking water, and some individuals prefer to reduce their exposure to it in their homes.
