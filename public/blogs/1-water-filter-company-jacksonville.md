### The Jacksonville Water Story: Sulfur, Iron, and Calcium

The Floridan Aquifer, while a robust water source, naturally introduces certain elements into Jacksonville's water supply. The most common culprits affecting taste and appearance are:

*   **Sulfur:** Often detected by a distinct "rotten egg" smell, hydrogen sulfide gas can be present in groundwater. While generally not a health risk at typical levels, it's highly unpleasant for drinking, cooking, and showering.
*   **Iron:** This mineral can cause reddish-brown staining on fixtures, laundry, and dishes. It can also give water a metallic taste.
*   **Calcium (Hardness):** High levels of calcium and magnesium lead to "hard water," resulting in scale buildup on pipes and appliances, reduced soap lathering, and dry skin and hair.

For many Jacksonville residents, these are not just minor inconveniences but daily frustrations that prompt a search for professional water treatment.
