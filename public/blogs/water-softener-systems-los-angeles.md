### The LA Water Story: Why Hardness Matters

Los Angeles's reliance on imported water, while essential for sustaining a sprawling metropolis, brings with it a predictable downside. The journey from distant rivers and aquifers picks up dissolved minerals like calcium and magnesium, leading to water hardness. While not a health concern, hard water is an economic and quality-of-life issue. Homeowners routinely grapple with:

*   **Limescale buildup:** Clogging pipes, showerheads, and reducing the efficiency of water heaters and dishwashers.
*   **Soap and detergent inefficiency:** Requiring more product to achieve cleanliness, leading to residue on dishes and clothes.
*   **Dry skin and hair:** Mineral deposits can strip natural oils, contributing to irritation.
*   **Reduced appliance lifespan:** Hard water minerals accelerate wear and tear on all water-using appliances.
