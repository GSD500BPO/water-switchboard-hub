### The NYC Water Paradox: Pristine Source, Local Concerns

New York's water system is a marvel of engineering, delivering water from protected watersheds without extensive filtration thanks to its high quality. However, the problem isn't with the source water itself; it's often with the *last mile* – the pipes within individual buildings. Many older structures throughout Manhattan, Brooklyn, Queens, and the Bronx still rely on lead service lines or internal plumbing that can leach lead into drinking water, particularly after water has sat stagnant for several hours. This is a significant concern, as even low levels of lead exposure can have serious health implications, especially for children.

Beyond lead, aging pipes can also contribute to issues like sediment, rust particles, and even an altered taste or odor, impacting everything from your morning coffee to your shower experience. For businesses, especially those in hospitality or food service, consistent high-quality water is not just a preference but a necessity for operations and customer satisfaction.
