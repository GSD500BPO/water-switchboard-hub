### The Austin Water Landscape and the RO Solution

The "moderately hard" designation for Austin's water means it contains elevated levels of minerals like calcium and magnesium. These minerals, while not harmful to health, are responsible for scale buildup in pipes and appliances, spots on dishes, and can impact the taste of drinking water. Seasonal fluctuations can further exasperate these issues, bringing varying concentrations of dissolved solids.

Reverse osmosis systems work by forcing water through a semi-permeable membrane, effectively filtering out microscopic impurities, dissolved salts, and even some pharmaceuticals and chemicals that standard filtration might miss.
