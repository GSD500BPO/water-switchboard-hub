### Addressing Jacksonville's Unique Water Profile

The Floridan Aquifer, while a robust source, presents distinct characteristics:
*   **Sulfur:** Often manifests as a "rotten egg" smell, particularly noticeable when water has been sitting. This can be off-putting for drinking, cooking, and showering.
*   **Iron:** Leads to reddish-brown staining on fixtures, laundry, and dishes, and can impart a metallic taste to water.
*   **Calcium (Hardness):** Causes limescale buildup in pipes, water heaters, and appliances, reducing their efficiency and lifespan. It also makes soaps and detergents less effective, leading to drier skin and hair.
