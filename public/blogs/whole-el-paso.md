**El Paso's Water: A Hard-Fought Battle**

The Hueco Bolson aquifer, a vital groundwater source, naturally contains a significant concentration of dissolved minerals, primarily calcium and magnesium. Similarly, water from the Rio Grande, after its long journey and interaction with various geological formations, also contributes to the region's overall hard water profile and elevated salt levels. These minerals, while not typically a health hazard, can wreak havoc on plumbing, appliances, and even the taste of your drinking water. From shortening the lifespan of water heaters to making soaps less effective, the effects of hard water are widely felt across the city.
