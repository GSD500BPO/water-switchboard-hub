### Atlanta's Water Story: Softness with a Catch

The Chattahoochee River generally supplies Atlanta with relatively soft water, which means fewer issues with hard water scaling that plague many other cities. However, the municipal treatment process, essential for eradicating harmful pathogens, relies on chlorine. While chlorine effectively purifies, it can react with organic matter in the water to form those aforementioned **chlorine disinfection byproducts (DBPs)**.

DBPs, while regulated by the EPA, can contribute to an unpleasant taste and odor in tap water. More importantly, long-term exposure to certain DBPs has been linked to potential health concerns. This is a primary reason why countless Atlanta homeowners and businesses seek advanced filtration beyond what their municipal provider offers.
