## Jacksonville's Water: Decoding the Floridan Aquifer with Drinking Water Filtration

Jacksonville, a sprawling city known for its beautiful rivers and coastal charm, relies heavily on the Floridan Aquifer for its water supply. While this ancient underground reservoir provides abundant water, it comes with its own set of natural characteristics that can impact household water quality. Residents often encounter issues stemming from naturally occurring minerals like **sulfur**, which can cause an unpleasant "rotten egg" smell; **iron**, leading to reddish-brown staining and metallic tastes; and **calcium**, the primary culprit behind hard water and limescale buildup.
