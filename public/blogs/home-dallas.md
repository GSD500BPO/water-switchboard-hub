### Understanding Dallas's Water Landscape

The most common concern for Dallas residents is water hardness. Our local water typically registers between **8 and 12 grains per gallon (GPG)**, placing it firmly in the "moderately hard" to "hard" category. This isn't just a number; it translates directly into visible problems: soap scum on shower doors, mineral deposits on faucets and dishes, and reduced efficiency and lifespan of water-using appliances like water heaters and dishwashers.

Beyond hardness, Dallas water experiences **seasonal fluctuations in chlorine levels and turbidity**. While chlorine is essential for disinfecting municipal water, its presence can impact the taste and smell of drinking water. Turbidity, or cloudiness, indicates the presence of suspended solids, which, while generally filtered at the plant, can still be a concern for those seeking the purest possible water.
