### The Realities of Orlando's Water Quality

As you might already know, Orlando's primary water source is groundwater drawn from the Floridan Aquifer. While generally safe to drink, this source is naturally rich in minerals. This leads to **elevated water hardness**, a common culprit behind limescale buildup on fixtures, cloudy dishes, and stiff laundry. Hard water can also reduce the lifespan of appliances and make soaps less effective, leaving skin and hair feeling dry.

Beyond hardness, many Orlando residents contend with the unmistakable **sulfur odor**, often described as a "rotten egg" smell. This occurs when hydrogen sulfide gas is present in the water, a naturally occurring byproduct of certain bacteria and geological conditions. While usually not a health risk, it's certainly an unpleasant nuisance that can permeate your home.

Furthermore, the presence of dissolved **iron** is another frequent complaint, leading to unsightly reddish-brown staining on sinks, toilets, and laundry. This can be particularly frustrating for homeowners striving to maintain a clean and pristine living environment.
