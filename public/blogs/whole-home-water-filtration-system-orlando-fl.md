### Orlando's Water Story: Beyond the Tap

Before diving into solutions, it's crucial to understand the problems. The high mineral content in Orlando's groundwater leads to **hard water**, manifesting as soap scum, dry skin, and limescale buildup in pipes and appliances. The unpleasant **sulfur odor**, often described as a "rotten egg" smell, is caused by hydrogen sulfide gas and can be particularly off-putting. Finally, the presence of **iron and manganese** can lead to reddish-brown staining on fixtures, laundry, and even affect the taste of drinking water. These are the primary issues that local water treatment companies are called upon to resolve.
