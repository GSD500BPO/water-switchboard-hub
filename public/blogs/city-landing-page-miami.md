### Understanding Miami's Water: From Biscayne Aquifer to Your Tap

The vast majority of South Florida's drinking water originates from the **Biscayne Aquifer**. While this natural underground reservoir provides a plentiful supply, it comes with its own set of characteristics that often impact home water quality. Miami residents frequently contend with:

*   **Elevated Chlorine Levels:** Essential for disinfection, chlorine leaves many homes with a distinct swimming pool smell and taste that can be off-putting for drinking and cooking.
*   **Limestone Minerals (Hard Water):** The aquifer's limestone bedrock means Miami's water is naturally hard. This manifests as mineral buildup on fixtures, scale in appliances, dull laundry, and dry skin and hair.
*   **Occasional Algae Byproducts:** During certain times of the year, algae blooms in source waters can lead to earthy or musty tastes and odors, even after treatment.

These common issues underscore the need for effective home water treatment, and this is precisely where City Landing Page focuses its expertise.
