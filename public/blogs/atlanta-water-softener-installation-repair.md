### Navigating Atlanta's Water Landscape

The Chattahoochee River provides Atlanta with a reliable water source, and the municipal treatment plants work diligently to make it safe for consumption. The "soft" nature of the water means fewer issues with hard water stains or scale buildup compared to many other regions. However, the necessary use of chlorine for disinfection, while vital for public health, can lead to the formation of DBPs. These compounds, such as trihalomethanes (THMs) and haloacetic acids (HAAs), are regulated by the EPA due to potential long-term health concerns. For many Atlantans, the desire to reduce exposure to these byproducts, as well as improve the taste and odor of their tap water, drives the decision to invest in home water filtration.
