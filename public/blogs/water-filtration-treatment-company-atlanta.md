**The Atlanta Water Challenge: Beyond "Soft" Water**

Atlanta's water is generally considered soft, which is a plus for preventing scale buildup compared to hard water regions. However, the use of chlorine as a primary disinfectant, while vital for public health, inevitably leads to the formation of DBPs. These byproducts occur when chlorine reacts with naturally occurring organic matter in the water. For homeowners and businesses in Atlanta, this often translates into a distinct "chlorine" smell or taste, particularly noticeable in drinking water or during showers. More importantly, understanding and addressing DBPs is a growing priority for those seeking the highest quality water for their families.
