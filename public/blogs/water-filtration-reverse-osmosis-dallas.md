**Understanding Dallas's Water Landscape**

The municipal water in Dallas typically registers as moderately hard, ranging between 8-12 grains per gallon (GPG). For many, this translates to common household frustrations: stubborn soap scum, mineral buildup on fixtures and appliances, and a feeling of residue on skin and hair after showering. Beyond hardness, Dallas water also experiences seasonal fluctuations in chlorine levels. While chlorine is essential for disinfection, higher concentrations can lead to noticeable tastes and odors, potentially impacting drinking water quality and even causing skin sensitivity for some.

It's these everyday experiences that drive many Dallas residents and businesses to explore water treatment options.
