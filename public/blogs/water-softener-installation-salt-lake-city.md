### Understanding Salt Lake City's Water Challenge

The water flowing through the Wasatch Front comes primarily from two sources: mountain snowmelt and underground aquifers. While these sources provide clean, fresh water, they also contribute to its moderate hardness. As water travels through rock and soil, it picks up minerals like calcium and magnesium. In Salt Lake City, this results in an average water hardness of **12-15 grains per gallon (gpg)**.
