### The Hard Truth About Orlando's Water

Imagine stepping out of the shower feeling sticky, or watching your new dishwasher struggle with spots on every glass. These are common experiences for Orlando residents dealing with hard water. The high mineral content – primarily calcium and magnesium – leaves behind scale buildup in pipes, on faucets, and inside water-using appliances. Over time, this buildup can reduce efficiency, increase energy bills, and even lead to expensive repairs or premature appliance replacement.

Beyond hardness, the notorious "rotten egg" smell of sulfur in tap water is another frequent complaint. While typically harmless, it's unpleasant and can make drinking, cooking, and bathing an uninviting experience. And for those with well water or particularly high iron levels in municipal sources, reddish-brown stains on sinks, toilets, and clothing are a constant battle.
