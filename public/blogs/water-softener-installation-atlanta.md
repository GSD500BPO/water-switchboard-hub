### Atlanta's Water Story: Softness, But With a Catch

Atlanta draws its primary water supply from the Chattahoochee River, a source generally known for producing relatively **soft water**. This means Atlantans typically don't experience the immediate issues associated with hard water, such as mineral buildup on fixtures or diminished soap lather. However, "soft" doesn't necessarily mean "pure" or "free from all concerns."

The purification process for municipal water often involves chlorine to disinfect and eliminate harmful bacteria. While vital for public health, this chlorination can lead to the formation of **chlorine disinfection byproducts (DBPs)**. These byproducts, along with the lingering taste and odor of chlorine itself, can impact the sensory experience of your tap water and raise questions for health-conscious consumers. For many, simply removing these elements can drastically improve the water they drink, cook with, and bathe in.
