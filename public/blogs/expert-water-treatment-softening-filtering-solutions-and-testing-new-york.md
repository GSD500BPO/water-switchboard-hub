### The NYC Water Story: Quality Source, Aging Delivery

New York's water system draws from a vast network of upstate reservoirs, providing naturally soft water that requires minimal treatment before reaching the city. This natural softness means many residents don't experience the hard water issues (like scale buildup or soap scum) common in other parts of the country.

However, the primary concern for many New Yorkers is the potential for **lead contamination**. While the city's water does not contain lead when it leaves treatment plants, it can pick up lead from old service lines and internal plumbing in older buildings. This often goes unnoticed until a resident experiences health issues or decides to get their water tested.
